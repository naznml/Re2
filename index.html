Мәтін Текст

B1+ Reading Practice · University Life

Topic: The University of Life

The majority of eighteen-year-old students entering higher education go straight from school to university. For many school leavers, however, there is the irresistible attraction of the “gap year”, a time between school and university when they decide to experience something new, different or exciting.

Many of these so-called “gappers” go travelling around the world, often supplementing their limited funds by taking on casual work. Others may do voluntary work in a village in a distant part of the world. For the majority of gappers, the gap year is simply a chance to enjoy life as an independent adult for the first time.

Increasingly, however, gap years are also proving a useful way of renewing a lapsed or weakening interest in education. They offer young people a chance to think about why they should study, or whether they need to study at all. A growing number of students, having taken a break after school, are heading back into further and higher education through a less direct route of working and gapping.

According to recent data from the British university admissions service, UCAS, 105,000 students aged nineteen and 44,400 students aged twenty entered higher education last year. These figures show a steady annual increase in this age group over the previous three years.

Nineteen-year-old student Christine Samways is a typical example. She left school at sixteen with nine good exam passes at grades A to C, but she did not want to continue studying at the time. She was also worried that, despite having the qualities of a good student, she would find the challenges of higher education too great and would be forced to drop out.

Reading strategy

  • Main idea (негізгі ой) сұрағында жеке story емес, бүкіл passage (мәтін) идеясын таңда.
  • Vocabulary in context (контекст арқылы сөз мағынасы) үшін сөздің алдындағы және кейінгі сөйлемді оқы.
  • Inference (қорытынды жасау) сұрағында figures (сандар) немесе cause-effect (себеп-салдар) байланысын түсін.
  • В main idea (главная мысль) выбирай не отдельную story, а идею всего passage (текста).
  • Для vocabulary in context (значение по контексту) читай предложение до и после нужного слова.
  • В inference (вывод) понимай figures (цифры) или cause-effect (причинно-следственную) связь.

Мәтін Текст

B1+ Reading Practice · Scientific Work

Topic: The Scientific Method

Hypotheses are often described as imaginative and inspiring. They are “adventures of the mind” because they allow researchers to think beyond what is already known. Some philosophers of science have argued that the scientific method is not simply inductive, as is commonly believed, but hypothetico-deductive.

As a researcher, it is important to understand the difference between these two descriptions of the research process. Research does not always begin with a perfectly neutral observation of facts. Therefore, researchers should not feel that they are “cheating” if their work begins with an idea, expectation or possible explanation.

The common myth of the scientific method is that it is purely inductive. According to this view, scientific theory begins with simple, unbiased and impartial observation. Researchers collect raw sensory information, usually called facts, and then general theories somehow develop from this collection of information. However, this starting point is problematic.

There is no such thing as completely impartial observation. Every action we observe is influenced by what we have already seen, learned or experienced in the past. Scientific work of an experimental or research nature usually begins with certain expectations about the result. This expectation is called a hypothesis.

Hypotheses give direction and energy to investigation. They affect the methodology because, in the light of an expectation, some observations are considered relevant while others are seen as unrelated. One method is selected and another is rejected. Some experiments are carried out, while others are not. In this sense, the idea of a completely innocent, pure and objective researcher is difficult to defend.

Hypotheses may arise through conjecture or inspiration, but they must be formalized and tested carefully using a suitable method. If the predictions made from a hypothesis are not shown to be correct, the hypothesis should be changed or rejected. If the predictions are accurate, the hypothesis may be supported and kept until further tests show that it needs to be revised.

Мәтін Текст

B2 Reading Practice · Scientists and Society

Topic: Science and Human Life

Science and the techniques to which it has given rise have changed human life during the last hundred and fifty years more than it had been changed since human beings first took to agriculture. The changes brought about by science continue at increasing speed, and there is no sign that a new stable scientific plateau will soon be reached.

On the contrary, there is every reason to think that the revolutionary possibilities of science extend immeasurably beyond what has so far been realised. This raises an important question: can the human race adjust itself quickly enough to these rapid transformations, or will it, like many former species, perish from lack of adaptability?

The dinosaurs were once the lords of creation. If there had been philosophers among them, not one would have foreseen that the whole race might perish. Yet they became extinct because they could not adapt themselves to a world without swamps.

In the case of human beings and science, there is a completely new factor. Human beings themselves are creating the changes of environment to which they will have to adjust with unprecedented rapidity. Although human scientific skill is the cause of these environmental changes, most of them are not directly intended by human beings.

Even when changes come about through human actions, they can have something of the inevitability of natural forces. Whether nature dries up swamps or people deliberately drain them makes little difference to the final result. In both cases, living creatures must adapt or face serious consequences.

Whether human beings will be able to survive the changes of environment that their own skill has brought about remains an open question. If the answer is positive, people will have to apply scientific ways of thinking not only to nature, but also to themselves and to their social institutions.

Topic: Corporate Social Responsibility

Corporate Social Responsibility, or CSR, means that a company should think not only about profit, but also about people, society and the environment. A responsible company tries to do business in an honest and useful way.

There are several reasons why companies use CSR. The first reason is moral responsibility. This means that companies should “do the right thing”. They should respect workers, customers, local communities and nature. For example, a company should not harm the environment or treat people unfairly.

The second reason is sustainability. Sustainability means using resources carefully today, so that future generations can also use them. A company should not only think about short-term success. It should also think about the future. For example, it can save energy, reduce waste, recycle materials and support local communities.

The third reason is permission from society. Every company needs support from people, communities and governments. If a company damages the environment or ignores social problems, people may stop trusting it. But if a company helps society, people are more likely to support its work.

The fourth reason is reputation. CSR can improve a company’s image. When people see that a company is ethical and responsible, they may trust it more. This can make the company’s brand stronger and improve the motivation of its workers.

In short, CSR is important because business affects many parts of life. A good company should make money, but it should also respect people, protect the environment and help society.

B1+ Reading Practice · Famous Scientists

Topic: Marie Curie and the Value of Scientific Research

Marie Curie is remembered as one of the most important scientists in modern history. Her work helped change the study of physics and chemistry, and it also influenced medicine. She became famous for her research on radioactivity, a form of energy that scientists were only beginning to understand at the end of the nineteenth century.

Marie Curie was born in Warsaw in 1867. At that time, opportunities for women in higher education were limited in many places. Curie wanted to continue her studies, so she moved to Paris, where she studied physics and mathematics. Life in Paris was not easy. She had little money and often lived very simply, but she continued to work hard because she believed that education and science were important.

Her scientific career became closely connected with the study of invisible rays. In 1896, Henri Becquerel discovered that uranium gave off unusual radiation. Curie became interested in this discovery and decided to investigate it further. She carefully measured the radiation produced by different substances and noticed something unexpected. Some minerals seemed to give off much more radiation than uranium itself.

This observation led Curie and her husband, Pierre Curie, to continue their research. They believed that these minerals must contain unknown substances. After a long period of difficult work, they discovered two new elements: polonium and radium. These discoveries were important because they showed that matter contained forms of energy that had not been fully understood before.

Curie’s research was not easy or comfortable. She worked for many hours in poor laboratory conditions and had to process large amounts of material to find very small quantities of radioactive elements. Her work required patience, careful measurement and strong discipline. It also showed that scientific discovery is often the result of repeated experiments rather than a sudden moment of success.

In 1903, Marie Curie shared the Nobel Prize in Physics with Pierre Curie and Henri Becquerel for their work on radiation. Later, in 1911, she received the Nobel Prize in Chemistry for her work with radium. She became the first woman to win a Nobel Prize and the first person to win Nobel Prizes in two different scientific fields.

Curie’s discoveries later helped the development of medical science, especially in the use of radiation and X-ray technology. However, at that time, the dangers of radiation were not fully known. Curie’s own health was affected by long exposure to radioactive materials.

Today, Marie Curie is remembered not only for her discoveries, but also for her determination as a researcher. Her life shows that science needs curiosity, courage and responsibility. It also shows that one person’s research can open a new field of knowledge and influence society for many years.

Мәтін Текст

B1+ Reading Practice · Famous Scientists

Topic: Albert Einstein and Scientific Imagination

Albert Einstein is one of the most famous scientists in the history of modern physics. His name is often connected with intelligence, creativity and new ways of thinking about the universe. However, Einstein’s importance was not only the result of natural talent. His work also shows how scientific progress can come from curiosity, imagination and the courage to question accepted ideas.

Einstein was born in Germany in 1879. As a child, he was interested in how things worked. He was especially curious about light, space and time. He did not always enjoy traditional school methods, but he liked thinking deeply about difficult questions. This habit of asking simple but powerful questions later became one of the most important parts of his scientific work.

At the beginning of his career, Einstein did not have a famous university position. He worked in a patent office in Switzerland, where he examined new inventions. This job was not the same as working in a laboratory, but it gave him time to think. While doing ordinary work during the day, he continued to study physics and develop his own ideas.

In 1905, Einstein published several important scientific papers. One of them explained the photoelectric effect. This effect happens when light hits a material and causes small particles called electrons to leave its surface. Einstein’s explanation helped scientists understand that light could behave like small packets of energy. This idea later became important in the development of modern physics.

Another paper introduced the special theory of relativity. This theory changed the way scientists thought about time, space and motion. Before Einstein, many people believed that time and space were fixed and the same for everyone. Einstein showed that they could depend on motion. This was a difficult idea, but it opened a new way of understanding the physical world.

Einstein is also famous for the equation E = mc². The equation shows a relationship between energy and mass. Although it is short, it changed scientific ideas about matter and energy. It suggested that mass could be changed into energy, and that even a small amount of matter could contain a very large amount of energy.

Later, Einstein developed the general theory of relativity, which explained gravity in a new way. Instead of seeing gravity only as a force, Einstein described it as an effect of the shape of space and time. This theory was difficult for many people to understand at first, but later observations supported important parts of it.

In 1921, Einstein received the Nobel Prize in Physics, not for relativity, but for his explanation of the photoelectric effect. This shows an important point about science: a discovery may be accepted more easily when it can be tested and supported by evidence. Some of Einstein’s ideas were so new that they needed time before other scientists fully accepted them.

Einstein’s life shows that science is not only about memorising facts. It is also about asking questions, imagining possibilities and testing ideas carefully. His work changed physics, but his example also changed the image of a scientist. He showed that independent thinking can lead to discoveries that transform the way people understand the world.

  • В main idea (главная мысль) выбирай не один факт, а общий смысл всего passage (текста).
  • В purpose question (вопрос на цель) понимай, зачем автор использует example (пример): он часто подтверждает главную идею.

B1+ Reading Practice · Ecology and Technical Progress

Topic: Green Technology and Environmental Sustainability

Technology is often blamed for environmental problems, but it can also become part of the solution. In recent years, scientists, engineers and businesses have developed new tools to help people use energy, water and land more carefully. These tools are often called green technologies because they are designed to reduce harm to the environment and support a more sustainable way of living.

One important area of green technology is renewable energy. Solar panels and wind turbines allow people to produce electricity without burning coal, oil or gas. In the past, renewable energy was often expensive and less reliable. Today, however, many renewable systems have become more efficient and more affordable. This means that more homes, schools, companies and cities can use cleaner energy.

Another important development is the smart grid. A smart grid is an electricity system that uses digital technology to manage energy more effectively. It can help match energy supply with demand, reduce waste and support the use of renewable energy. For example, when solar panels produce extra electricity during the day, a smart system can help store or distribute that energy more efficiently.

Technology can also make cities more sustainable. Smart lighting systems can reduce energy use by turning lights on only when they are needed. Sensors can help control heating, cooling and traffic. In large cities, where millions of people use energy and transport every day, even small improvements can have a large environmental effect.

Agriculture is another area where technology can help. Precision farming uses data, sensors and sometimes satellite information to help farmers make better decisions. Instead of using the same amount of water or fertiliser everywhere, farmers can use resources only where they are needed. This can reduce waste, protect soil and lower the amount of chemicals entering rivers and lakes.

Green technology is also used in waste management. Recycling machines, waste-sorting systems and new materials can help reduce the amount of rubbish sent to landfills. Some companies are trying to design products that can be repaired, reused or recycled more easily. This is important because environmental sustainability is not only about producing clean energy; it is also about using fewer resources.

However, technology alone cannot solve every environmental problem. Producing electronic devices requires materials, energy and transport. Old devices can become electronic waste if they are not recycled safely. In addition, green technology can be expensive, and not every community has equal access to it.

Мәтін Текст

B1+ Reading Practice · Global Problems and Technology

Topic: Technology and Climate Change

Climate change is one of the most serious global problems of the twenty-first century. It is mainly caused by the increase of greenhouse gases in the atmosphere, especially carbon dioxide. These gases trap heat and make the planet warmer. As a result, many countries are looking for technological solutions that can reduce emissions and help societies adapt to a changing climate.

One of the most important solutions is the development of renewable energy. Solar, wind and hydroelectric power can produce electricity with far fewer emissions than coal, oil or gas. As renewable energy becomes cheaper, more countries are adding it to their energy systems. However, renewable energy also needs better storage, because the sun does not always shine and the wind does not always blow.

Energy storage is therefore a key part of climate technology. Batteries can store electricity when production is high and release it when demand increases. This can make renewable energy more reliable. At the same time, scientists are trying to develop batteries that last longer, charge faster and use materials that are less harmful to the environment.

Transport is another area where technology can reduce emissions. Electric vehicles produce no exhaust gases while they are being driven. If the electricity used to charge them comes from renewable sources, their environmental benefit becomes even greater. Public transport, cycling systems and better urban planning can also reduce the number of private cars on roads.

Some technologies aim to remove carbon dioxide from the air or stop it from entering the atmosphere. This is often called carbon capture. In some systems, carbon dioxide is collected from factories or power stations before it is released. In others, researchers try to remove it directly from the air. These methods may help reduce emissions, but they are still expensive and cannot replace the need to cut pollution at its source.

Technology can also help people adapt to climate change. Weather satellites, early warning systems and climate models can help governments prepare for floods, droughts and extreme heat. Farmers can use climate data to choose stronger crops and manage water more carefully. Cities can design cooler buildings, plant more trees and improve drainage systems.

Even with these tools, technology is not a complete answer. Climate change is connected with energy use, transport, food production and patterns of consumption. If people continue to use resources carelessly, technology will not be enough. Real progress requires political decisions, business responsibility and changes in everyday behaviour.

Reading strategy

  • Main idea (негізгі ой) сұрағында text (мәтін) тек technology туралы ма, әлде technology + limitation туралы ма — соны ажырат.
  • Inference (қорытынды жасау) сұрағында мәтіндегі нақты сөйлемді емес, logical meaning (логикалық мағына)-ны таңда.
  • Cause-effect (себеп-салдар байланысы) үшін because, however, therefore, as a result сияқты signal words (белгі сөздер)-ге қара.
  • В main idea (главная мысль) различай: текст только о technology или о technology + limitation.
  • В inference (вывод) выбирай не прямую фразу, а logical meaning (логический смысл).
  • Для cause-effect (причинно-следственная связь) отмечай signal words (сигнальные слова): because, however, therefore, as a result.
